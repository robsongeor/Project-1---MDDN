World to Wellington

The Internet wiggles and worms its way through out the planet.

But how on earth does it get to your home in Wellington?

This small and simple guide will illustrate the journey of networks and how us wellingtonians are able to connect from our homes, streets and cities to the rest of the world.

WORLD

Servers

Servers are where information is stored, such as websites or apps and even your information! Servers are scattered all around the world and they’re all independently owned, meaning no one entity is in control.

Cables

Underwater long distance cables known as fibre optic cables stretch all over the globe connecting country to country. Many different companies own and operate the cables, but they usually all meet at internet exchange points, creating are large network of cables. The cable that allows New Zealand to connect to the world is known as the Southern Cross Cable Network which stretches 30500 kilometres all the way from the United States!

CITY

Internet Exchange Points

New Zealand has multiple Internet Exchange Points scattered through out the main cities; Auckland, Wellington, Christchurch and Dunedin. These exchanges enable servers and cables to interconnect to internet service providers, such as Orcon and Spark.

Internet Service Providers

An Internet service provider is an organisation that provides services for accessing, using, or participating in the Internet. This is who you pay to access the internet. They buy access to the network of cables that run to your street and then to your house that is owned and operated by chorus. This cable network runs through out the country.

STREET

Chrous

Under the roads and streets of New Zealand lie large networks of copper and fibre cables. This network of cables is run by chorus, who sell the use of these cables to the Internet service providers. These cables run back to the exchanges.

HOME

Modem

A modem is a device that converts data sent through the chorus network from your Internet service provider into a digital signal ready for devices to receive. This device is what allows you to connect to the internet.

Router

A Router takes the digital signal from the modem to allow communication between devices and the Internet. The router has antenna that can encode and send data, and decode and receive data through radio waves. Though most home routers are wireless they do include wired ports that allow a physical connection through an ethernet cable.

Ethernet

Desktops and Older laptops sometimes don’t have the antennas to receive Wireless radio waves. These devices have to make use of a wired connection to send and receive data. The ethernet cable facilitates this transfer of data  

Wireless Devices

Most of us own mobile phones, laptops and tablets, and they almost allways include the ability to send and receive information wirelessly. Antenna in the devices is able decode information sent in the form radio waves transmitted by the wireless router.
